About Donu

Donu Kogbara

Donu Kogbara is a seasoned print/broadcast journalist and communications expert. She grew up in the UK, studied Combined Arts at the University of Leicester and started her career in London, where she worked for well-known media outlets such as the Economist Intelligence Unit, Sunday Times newspaper, High Life (British Airways in-flight magazine) and the BBC.

CAREER:
  • 2003 - 2006 - Special Assistant to Dr Abiye Sekibo, Minister of Transport
  • 2000 - 2009 - Member of the Presidential Oil/Gas Sector Reform Implementation Committee that drafted the original Petroleum Industry Bill
  • 2022 - 2023 - Member of the National Security Adviser and Presidential Amnesty Programme Administra for Oil Theft Investigation Panel
  • A member of the Greater Port Harcourt City Development Authority board (appointed by Rivers State Governor Rotimi Amaechi) for 6 years.

Donu continues to be a key media player. She often appears on and produces TV documentaries and news programmes, has a weekly column in Vanguard, a Nigerian paper and writes for The Africa Report, a Paris-based international magazine.

Also, She has also contributed to books including “New Daughters Of Africa,” a British anthology containing works by distinguished female writers (including Chimamanda and Zadie Smith) from all over Africa and the Diaspora.

African Access

Donu owns African Access, a world-class communications consultancy firm with many blue chip clients.
